A new daily habit takes a month to cement

So you want to start a new habit? The easiest way is to use a trigger, and start small. A trigger should be something you do every day, like: get out of bed, brush your teeth, use the bathroom or brew tea or coffee. Teach yourself to do the new thing when you hit the trigger. Every time you are about to brush your teeth, do one push up first, just one, to get into the habit. Don't worry about the effect. The first thing to do is to actually do you thing. Once the habit is in place, after a month, then you can increase your ambitions.

Flashcards will help you remember the details

There are so many things to remember. A friend of mine was studying to become a physician. They have to learn all the Latin names of all there bones in three body, by heart. And the organs. And the nerves, the glands and everything else. How do they do it? Flash cards help a lot. AnkiDroid automates this. AnkiDroid is an app for Android. I'm sure there is an equivalent for other phones.

My advice is to start small and make it personal. Everyone is different. I would keep at least two decks of cards: One for your domain or customer One for your technology

Whenever you encounter a concept that you think it would be beneficial to know by heart, add it to the appropriate deck. Don't be afraid to add more decks. It makes it easier to drop one group of cards should you lose that customer, or switch technology stack. Don't add too many cards, or out will be overwhelming. Remove cards that newcomer obsolete.

Cards can relate to concepts, products, people, places. Anything that would maker you look smarter if you knew it by heart.
